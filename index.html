About Us

The Bridge Papers is a groundbreaking experiment in citizen journalism. In an era when the power of the media grows in lockstep with the polarization of politics, the American people consistently seem to be divided between ourselves, due in part to the news sources we choose to consume and the issues we choose to care about. For most of us, the truth is that there are entirely hidden subcultures and issues around us, ones that we have never thought to explore.

In the fall of 2016, our team members (five students from central New Jersey) chose to adopt the resolve and vision necessary to share our worlds with the public. With topics ranging from flag design to the broken systems of American soccer, this first volume brings together eight parts of our global community. 'ALL FOR ONE,' the titular reference to Alexandre Dumas, draws every piece towards the theme of solidarity and self-improvement.

Our commitment to quality content extends beyond the pieces we wrote for this inaugural volume. We made a point to reach out to leaders in our community who bring unique perspectives to their work, and we would like to thank Dr. Louise Tokarsky-Unda, Dr. Micheal McDonough, and ______ for joining us in a better future. We are happy to announce that our New Media platform extends beyond our website, with an upcoming Instagram page and the homepage of our sibling organization, Project Break The Cycle. Our code is fully scripted in-house, and our open-source GitHub repository can be found here.

Jesus Rivera

Teach America's Children Politics

Oppressed perspectives and issues lie within the shadows of our society. Jesus’s work focuses around addressing those concerns. For the past 13 years of living in the United States, he has encountered the maladies that plague our society, while also experiencing the American privileges that the rest of the world envies. Jesus’s involvement in local government and social advocacy extends to his participation in the Bound Brook Democrats, the Bound Brook Special Improvement District, the Somerset County Youth Leadership Program, and El Foro Comunitario de Inmigracion.

‘To see injustice and do nothing about that means to participate in it’ - Jean-Jacques Rousseau

Sid Shankar

A 21st Century Flag for 21 Towns

Sid is honored to serve as the Publisher for this first volume of The Bridge Papers. Pulling together our team and community contacts to create this project was an amazing experience. He was fortunate enough to be an intern in Assemblyman Dr. Andrew Zwicker, PhD.’s office this past summer, and he found that growing more involved in the local political sphere was an invaluable journey. He has always had a great interest in government, technology, and design, and so creating a common home for all three has been a longstanding goal of Sid’s. He hopes to carry the standard of citizen journalism into the future, and sees unending potential in The Bridge Papers. Additionally, serving alongside Farouk Marhaba as Executive Director of Project Break The Cycle, collaborating with Noah Rodelius on noahrodeli.us, and playing a leadership role in his high school debate club have shown him more of the world and of his hometown than he previously thought possible. Here’s to another 16 years, and many more volumes of TBP.

Dr. Micheal McDonough, PhD.
Home, Again.
The current president of Raritan Valley Community College, his path in life has taken him from working on an oil rig to hitchhiking across the country. As RVCC puts it, "A native of Preston in Northwest England, Dr. Michael J. McDonough now resides in Hunterdon County with his wife, children, and two dogs (Jersey Girl and Quincy). The father of three and Buffalo Bills fan is an aficionado of golf, reading, travel, and theatre. He’s also an historical trivia buff and has a passion for classic rock, especially from the 1960s and ‛70s."
Noah Rodelius
Scout's Honor: Youth in International Soccer
Noah is the creative power behind noahrodeli.us, a blog dedicated to international soccer. Coming off his third high school varsity soccer season, he has recently joined FC Copa, a club soccer team based in New Jersey. He aims to attend NYU’s Stern School of Business and transition into sports management, namely soccer. Outside of school and sports, Noah is known as a Taco Bell fanatic and is currently in the process of expanding his repertoire of Tarantino movies.
Farouk Marhaba
Project Break The Cycle
Farouk Marhaba is the founder and Executive Director of Project Break The Cycle, an organization dedicated to the eradication of poverty from Lebanon. As a 16 year old, he also runs and manages several businesses, which include selling shoes as well as manufacturing custom clothing for clients. In addition, he actively pursues his interests in economics and medicine wherever he sees opportunities in these fields.
Ayush Shah
The Principle of Ahimsa
Over the past few years, Ayush has become increasingly attuned to the injustices that plague our world. He aims to uncover these hidden truths to create a more aware society and to prevent the ignorance that he was oblivious to in his past. Outside of The Bridge Papers, Ayush is involved in Project Break the Cycle. Now entering his third year of varsity basketball, Ayush strives to improve upon communication and networking abilities through team sports. Furthermore, he is involved in Model United Nations, along with Uplift Humanity, BRHS Photography Club, and BRHS Track and Field. As of now, Ayush is spending his free time improving his photography and learning how to play guitar. In the future, he plans to pursue a career in neurosurgery supplemented with a bachelor’s in business.
Dr. Louise Tokarsky-Unda, PhD.
Combing the Snakes from our Hair
Dr. Tokarsky-Unda is a cultural anthropologist with years of teaching experience and a background in migration studies, alongside an interest in native cultures of the Americas.
